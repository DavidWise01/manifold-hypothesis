THE MANIFOLD

ENTELÉCHEIA · a theory of emergence · 1991 → now
⬡ Book VII of VIII · 6 emergents · 4 chapters

The geometric theory: real data — images, language — doesn't fill its huge space; it lies on a low-dimensional, smooth manifold inside it. Nets work by learning that surface, and they generalize because it's smooth. Emergence is the discovery of the shape of the world hidden in the raw dimensions. A long-standing, foundational idea (Bengio and the representation-learning line).
